IELTS Full Practice Test | Listening and Reading

Overview of Full Practice Tests

Purpose
➧ Simulate real IELTS test conditions to evaluate performance and identify areas for improvement.

Components
➧ Includes both Listening and Reading sections.

Timing
➧ Allocate time similar to the actual test to get accustomed to the test environment.


Listening Practice Test

Format
➧ The Listening section consists of four sections with a total of 40 questions.

Timing
➧ 30 minutes of listening plus 10 minutes to transfer answers.

Types of Questions
➧ Multiple choice, matching, sentence completion, and short answer questions.

Instructions
1. Listen to the provided audio clips for Sections 1 and 2.
2. Answer the questions based on the information from the recordings.
3. Review and discuss answers after completing the listening exercise.

Section 1
➧ Conversation Between Two People Arranging a Holiday



Sample Questions
1. What dates are being considered for the holiday?
2. What destination is being discussed?

Section 2
➧ Monologue Providing Information About a Local Event



Sample Questions
1. What are the opening times of the event?
2. What activities are scheduled for the event?


Reading Practice Test

Format
➧ The Reading section includes three passages with a total of 40 questions.

Timing
➧ 60 minutes.

Types of Questions
➧ Multiple choice
➧ True | False | Not given
➧ Matching headings
➧ and Sentence completion

Instructions
1. Read the provided passages.
2. Answer the questions related to each passage.
3. Review and discuss answers after completing the reading exercise.

Passage 1
➧ A description of a historical landmark.

The Colosseum, located in Rome, Italy, is one of the most famous landmarks in the world. Constructed between AD 70 and AD 80, it was used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. The Colosseum could hold up to 80,000 spectators and featured a complex system of vaults and arches. Despite damage from earthquakes and stone robbers, it remains an iconic symbol of ancient Rome and attracts millions of visitors each year.

Sample Questions
1. When was the Colosseum originally built?
2. What was the Colosseum used for?

Passage 2
➧ An article about recent advancements in technology.

Recent advancements in technology have significantly transformed various aspects of daily life. Innovations such as smartphones, smart home devices, and artificial intelligence have made communication, home management, and information access more efficient. For instance, smartphones now integrate multiple functions, including cameras, GPS, and internet access, into a single device. Meanwhile, smart home devices allow users to control lighting, heating, and security systems remotely. These technological improvements continue to evolve, promising even greater convenience in the future.

Sample Questions
1. What are some functions integrated into modern smartphones?
2. How do smart home devices benefit users?

Passage 3
➧ A report on environmental conservation efforts.

Environmental conservation efforts are crucial for preserving natural habitats and biodiversity. Various strategies are being employed, including the establishment of protected areas, reforestation projects, and wildlife conservation programs. Protected areas, such as national parks, help safeguard ecosystems from human interference. Reforestation projects aim to restore deforested areas, while wildlife conservation programs work to protect endangered species through habitat preservation and anti-poaching measures. These initiatives are essential for maintaining ecological balance and supporting global biodiversity.

Sample Questions
1. What strategies are being used for conservation?
2. What is the goal of wildlife conservation programs?
