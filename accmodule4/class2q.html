First 60 units @ $5
Next 40 units @ $6

Step 2 - Calculate COGS
60 × $5 = $300
40 × $6 = $240
COGS = $540

Step 3 - Calculate Ending Inventory
Remaining units ➧ 40 units @ $6
Ending Inventory = $240


FIFO Thinking | Sample Answer

Explain in 3-4 sentences

➛ Why grocery stores prefer FIFO

➛ How FIFO helps reduce inventory spoilage

Grocery stores prefer FIFO because it ensures older products are sold before newer ones, reducing spoilage and waste. FIFO matches the physical flow of perishable goods. It also shows inventory at recent market prices, making financial statements more accurate.

⚠ Answers may vary but should include spoilage reduction, physical flow, and accurate valuation.

Questions

Which inventory valuation method is suitable?
➛ FIFO

Why does FIFO match the physical flow of goods?
➛ FIFO assumes older inventory is sold first, which reflects how goods actually move on shelves.

How does FIFO affect profit during inflation?
➛ FIFO results in lower COGS and higher gross profit during inflation.

How does FIFO improve balance sheet accuracy?
➛ Ending inventory is valued at recent purchase prices, giving a more realistic asset value.
