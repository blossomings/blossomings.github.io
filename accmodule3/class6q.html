Allowance for Doubtful Accounts

Quick Quiz

Q. What is the purpose of the allowance for doubtful accounts?
A. The purpose of the Allowance for Doubtful Accounts is to estimate the portion of Accounts Receivable that may not be collected, ensuring that receivables are reported at their net realizable value.

Q. Why is the direct write-off method not acceptable under GAAP?
A. The purpose of the Allowance for Doubtful Accounts is to estimate the portion of Accounts Receivable that may not be collected, ensuring that receivables are reported at their net realizable value.

Q. Is the allowance account an asset, liability, or expense?
A. The Allowance for Doubtful Accounts is a contra-asset account that reduces Accounts Receivable on the balance sheet.

Q. Which principle is satisfied by the allowance method?
A. The Matching Principle is satisfied because bad debt expense is recorded in the same period as the related credit sales.
