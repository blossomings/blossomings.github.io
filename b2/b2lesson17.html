• Practice talking about technological advancements and their potential impact

Technology and Innovations

Q. Why is it important?

A. Technology is constantly evolving, and discussing future innovations is key in many fields, from business to science. Being able to use future forms effectively will help in talking about developments and predictions.

Key Topics:
◦ Using future forms to talk about upcoming technologies and innovations

◦ Expressing predictions, plans, and ongoing future actions

Examples:
◦ AI will revolutionize the way we work.

◦ By 2030, we will be living in smart cities.

◦ I’m going to buy the latest smartphone when it’s released.

Grammar Focus

Will:
To make predictions or express decisions made at the moment of speaking

Going to:
To talk about plans or intentions for the future

Future Continuous:
To talk about actions that will be happening at a specific time in the future

Examples
Will:
Virtual reality will become more accessible.

Going to:
They’re going to develop a new AI assistant.

Future Continuous:
We’ll be interacting with AI on a daily basis in the future.

Vocabulary

Artificial Intelligence:
The simulation of human intelligence by machines

Automation:
The use of technology to perform tasks without human intervention

Innovation:
The introduction of new ideas, products, or methods

Breakthrough:
A significant discovery or development

Revolutionize:
To completely change the way something is done

Phrases to Use
⚬ We’re on the verge of a breakthrough in…
⚬ This innovation will revolutionize the industry…
⚬ Automation is going to change the way we work…

Wrap-Up

Homework:
Write a short essay (150-200 words) about a future technology you think will have a significant impact on society. Use at least two future forms in your writing.

Example:
Automation is going to revolutionize the workplace. In the future, many tasks that are currently done by humans will be performed by machines. By 2030, we will be working alongside robots and AI systems that handle routine tasks, allowing us to focus on more creative and strategic work.
