C Sound Guide

➧ Before a | o | u = /k/

Examples
➧ casa
➧ comer
➧ cuna


➧ Before e | i = "th" or "s"

Spain = /θ/
➧ cebra
➧ cine

Latin American = /s/
➧ cebra
➧ cine


G Sound Guide

➧ Before a | o | u = hard g

Examples
➧ gato
➧ goma
➧ gusano


➧ Before e | i = h sound

➧ gente
➧ girafa


GU Sound Guide

➧ Before e | i | u is usually silent, making a hard g

Examples
➧ guerra
➧ guitarra


GÜ Sound Guide

➧ The ü means the u is pronounced

Examples
➧ pingüino
➧ vergüenza


Q Sound Guide

➧ Always followed by ue or ui; sounds like k

Examples
➧ queso
➧ quien


R Sound Guide

➧ One r between vowels = soft tap

Examples
➧ caro
➧ pero


➧ Double rr = strong roll

Examples
➧ perro
➧ carro


H Sound Guide

➧ H is always silent

Examples
➧ hola
➧ hermano
➧ hielo


J Sound Guide

➧ Always pronounced like a strong English h

Examples
➧ jamón
➧ jardín
➧ jugo


LL Sound Guide

➧ Usually sounds like y (varies by region)

Examples
➧ llama
➧ lluvia


Y Sound Guide

➧ Alone or at the end = ee

Examples
➧ y
➧ soy

➧ at the beginning = y

Example
➧ yo


Ñ Sound Guide

➧ Sounds like ny in "canyon"

Examples
➧ niño
➧ mañana


V Sound Guide

➧ Pronounced almost the same as B in Spanish

Examples
➧ vaca
➧ vivir


Z Sound Guide

➧ Sounds like s (Latin America) or th (Spain)

Examples
➧ zapato
➧ zorro


X Sound Guide

➧ Usually ks

Examples
➧ examen
➧ taxi

➧ sometimes h in a few words

Example
➧ México
